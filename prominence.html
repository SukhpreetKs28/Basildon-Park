Basildon Park is famous for being one of the few houses of its kind to survive the impact of the 20th century on aristocratic life and is now a popular filming location and tourist attraction, finding new fame as a popular filming location. Its preserved Georgian interiors have made it ideal for period dramas, appearing as Netherfield Park in Pride & Prejudice (2005) and as Lady Grantham’s London residence, Grantham House, in Downton Abbey. The house has also featured in The Duchess and Marie Antoinette; its restorative efforts to preserve the 18th century make it a perfect place for a historical setting.

When filming films and TV shows, it is important to protect the house from any damage or changes, which is why special effects, camera angles, and tricks are used. The 2005 Pride and Prejudice was one of the most ambitious shoots undertaken by the National Trust. The camera follows multiple guests at the Netherfield Ball through a series of interlocking rooms, all in one shot, changing direction to follow different guests. To protect any furniture that couldn’t be moved, ornate mirrors were protected in situ and then hidden behind prop walls; rooms within rooms were built; and lighting was hidden in false columns. To remove one carpet took twenty people and a scissor lift that took it out of the Loggia, and pieces of ‘flattage’ were used to protect items that couldn't be moved and were used to transform the Octagon Room. In Bridgerton, where the filming was centred in the garden, the ‘greens’ department brought 5,000 artificial flowers that transformed the space to create a backdrop to transform the scene. In addition to this, there were strict rules on lighting, with one side of the house having a complete ban due to being home to nests of bats.

These use of techniques to protect the house is essential as it also means that it is safe to film at the historical houses causing them to gain more recognition in popular culture which would in turn increase the value of sites and the number of tourists as can be seen on the Ground Floor of Basildon House where there is an entire room dedicated to the filming and shoots that have occurred on the site.
